Pythagoras was an ancient Greek mathematician and philosopher, best known for the Pythagorean Theorem. This fundamental principle in geometry states that in a right-angled triangle, the square of the length of the hypotenuse (the side opposite the right angle) is equal to the sum of the squares of the lengths of the other two sides. Mathematically:

c² = a² + b²

This theorem is widely used in mathematics, physics, and engineering to calculate distances and solve geometric problems.
